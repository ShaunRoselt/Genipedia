# Genipedia

Genipedia is a home for family stories, a place to discover, document, and
celebrate the people who make up your family. It blends collaborative family
trees with wiki-style profile pages so every life, relationship, and memory
can be recorded, sourced, and shared.

What Genipedia does

- Build living family trees: visually connect relatives, trace branches, and
	follow relationships across generations.
- Create rich person pages: biographies, timelines, photos, documents, and
	memories live together on a single, community-maintained profile.
- Preserve sources and stories: attach citations, records, and first-hand
	remembrances so histories remain verifiable and meaningful.
- Collaborate with family: invite relatives to contribute, suggest edits,
	and keep family knowledge alive and growing.
- Respect privacy: control what information is public, private, or family-only
	so sensitive details stay protected.

Who it's for

- Families who want to preserve memories and pass them on.
- Genealogy enthusiasts seeking a collaborative, sourced approach.
- Historians and educators assembling biographical data and local histories.

Our values

- Community-driven: histories get richer when families and researchers
	contribute together.
- Verifiable: facts are supported by sources whenever possible.
- Privacy-first: living people’s details are treated with care and choice.
- Accessible: family histories should be easy to read, discover, and share.
